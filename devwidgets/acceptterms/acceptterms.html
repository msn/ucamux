Privacy defaults in the Academic Collaboration Environment

Your user ID (CRSID for University of Cambridge users), first name and last name comprise your Basic Profile Information in the Academic Collaboration Environment. Because the Academic Collaboration Environment is intended to enable collaboration and engagement outside as well as inside the University, Basic Profile Information is visible to anyone visiting the website, not just logged in users or members of the University.

University of Cambridge users logging in to the Academic Collaboration Environment via Raven will on first login be given the option to import their first name and last name automatically from the University Lookup Directory. You may opt out of this by making the appropriate selection. Not including your name in your Basic Profile Information will prevent anyone visiting the Academic Collaboration Environment website from finding you by searching on your name. You will be discoverable only by your CRSID. Conversely, including your name means that if someone searches for your name, they will also be able to see your CRSID.

Non-University of Cambridge users (“Friends”) are required to give their name.

By default, files you upload, groups you belong to, and profile information that you provide will be visible to anyone with an Academic Collaboration Environment account. These accounts will be available to members of the university and invited external members. This means that unless you deliberately change who can see a given piece of information, the information will be visible to Academic Collaboration Environment account holders. When you become a member of a public group or site, or participate in one, you should be aware that you are acting in the public domain.
